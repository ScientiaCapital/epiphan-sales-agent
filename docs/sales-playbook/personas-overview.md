## AV Director

**Who they are:** Manages 50-500+ classroom/meeting spaces with small teams.

**Title variations to look for:**
- Director of AV Services
- Classroom Technology Manager
- Director of Learning Spaces
- AV Manager

**Reports to:** CIO, VP of IT, VP of Facilities

**Budget authority:** $200K - $2M annually

### Pain Points

| Pain | Emotional Impact | How We Solve It |
|------|------------------|-----------------|
| Equipment failures during critical events | Anxiety, embarrassment | Hardware reliability - no PC crashes |
| Can't troubleshoot remotely | Frustration, wasted travel | Fleet management dashboard |
| Understaffed for room count | Overwhelm, burnout | Remote management reduces on-site needs |
| Legacy equipment aging out | Planning stress | Modern, supportable platform |

### Hot Buttons

- "Manage 200+ rooms from one dashboard"
- "NC State manages 300+ with minimal staff"
- "No recurring fees - ever"
- "3-year warranty with advance replacement"

### Discovery Questions

1. How many rooms/spaces do you currently manage?
2. How many people on your team handle AV support?
3. What's your biggest reliability pain point right now?
4. Can you troubleshoot remotely, or do you have to be on-site?
5. When's your next major refresh or construction project?

### Buying Signals

**High intent:**
- "We have a construction project starting..."
- "I can't keep managing this many rooms with this team size"
- "We had a major failure during [important event]"

**Medium intent:**
- "What's the support like?"
- "How does it integrate with our control system?"

## L&D Director

**Who they are:** Leads training programs, manages content creation workflows.

**Title variations to look for:**
- VP of Talent Development
- Chief Learning Officer
- Director of Learning & Development
- Training Director

**Reports to:** CHRO, VP of HR, COO

**Budget authority:** $500K - $5M+ annually

### Pain Points

| Pain | Emotional Impact | How We Solve It |
|------|------------------|-----------------|
| Can't scale content creation | Frustration, bottleneck | Self-service capture = more content |
| High production costs | Budget pressure | No ongoing production team needed |
| Inconsistent quality across locations | Brand/quality concerns | Standardized hardware |
| SME time is expensive/limited | Dependency, scheduling | Record once, deploy forever |

### Hot Buttons

- "218% higher revenue per employee with video training" (Brandon Hall)
- "Record SME once, train 1,000 employees"
- "Self-service capture - no production team required"
- "OpenAI uses Pearl as the workhorse of their streams"

### Discovery Questions

1. How are you creating training content today?
2. How much SME time do you spend on training delivery?
3. What's your cost per learner, and how are you trying to reduce it?
4. What happens when a key expert leaves or retires?

### Buying Signals

**High intent:**
- "We're trying to scale our training capacity"
- "Our production backlog is [X] months"
- "Key experts are retiring and we're losing knowledge"

## Technical Director

**Who they are:** Runs live productions for churches or events, often with volunteers.

**Title variations to look for:**
- Technical Director
- Production Director
- Media Director
- Broadcast Engineer

**Reports to:** AV Manager, Executive Pastor, Production Manager

**Budget authority:** $20K - $200K (recommends, may not approve)

### Pain Points

| Pain | Emotional Impact | How We Solve It |
|------|------------------|-----------------|
| Equipment failure during live event | Panic, embarrassment | Hardware doesn't crash like PCs |
| Volunteer turnover | Constant retraining | Touchscreen = 5-minute training |
| Complex setups | Stress before events | All-in-one simplicity |
| Can't fail on Sunday | Weekly anxiety | Proven reliability |

### Hot Buttons

- "Hardware doesn't crash like PCs"
- "5-minute volunteer training"
- "Touchscreen operation"
- "Can't fail on Sunday morning"

### Discovery Questions

1. What's your current production setup?
2. Who operates during events - staff or volunteers?
3. What's your biggest Sunday morning (or event day) worry?
4. Have you had equipment failures during a service or event?

### Buying Signals

**High intent:**
- "We had a [failure] during [important event]"
- "I can't keep training new volunteers"
- "We're building/renovating our [space]"

## Simulation Director

**Who they are:** Runs simulation center for medical/nursing education.

**Title variations to look for:**
- Director of Simulation
- Simulation Center Manager
- Director of Clinical Simulation

**Reports to:** Dean of Medicine, CMO, VP of Academic Affairs

**Budget authority:** $100K - $1M annually

### Pain Points

| Pain | Emotional Impact | How We Solve It |
|------|------------------|-----------------|
| Debriefing without video is less effective | Frustration | Multi-source video capture |
| HIPAA compliance with cloud recording | Legal/compliance anxiety | Local recording - PHI never leaves network |
| SSH/LCME accreditation prep | Stress, documentation burden | Reliable capture = evidence |

### Hot Buttons

- "Local recording - HIPAA compliant, no PHI in cloud"
- "SSH accreditation support"
- "Native SimCapture/LearningSpace integration"
- "One device for entire sim room"

### Discovery Questions

1. How are you capturing simulation sessions today?
2. Do you use SimCapture or LearningSpace?
3. When's your next SSH or LCME accreditation visit?
4. Are you concerned about HIPAA compliance with video storage?

### Buying Signals

**High intent:**
- "Our SSH visit is coming up in [X] months"
- "We're expanding/building a new simulation center"
- "We're concerned about [HIPAA/compliance]"

## Court Administrator

**Who they are:** Manages court operations, handles compliance with recording requirements.

**Title variations to look for:**
- Court Administrator
- Court Executive Officer
- Clerk of Court
- Trial Court Administrator

**Reports to:** Presiding Judge, Chief Justice, County Board

**Budget authority:** $100K - $5M annually

### Pain Points

| Pain | Emotional Impact | How We Solve It |
|------|------------------|-----------------|
| Court reporter shortage (critical nationwide) | Operational crisis | Electronic recording as supplement |
| Case backlog due to transcript delays | Stress, public pressure | Faster recording = faster transcripts |
| Open Meeting Law compliance | Legal risk | Reliable public meeting recording |
| ADA accessibility requirements | Compliance anxiety | Captioning capability |

### Hot Buttons

- "Address court reporter shortage"
- "33 states permit electronic recording in courts"
- "1.78M hearings go without verbatim record annually"
- "Available through TIPS and Sourcewell cooperative purchasing"

### Discovery Questions

1. How are you handling the court reporter shortage?
2. What percentage of hearings have verbatim recording today?
3. What's your transcript turnaround time?
4. Have you received ADA complaints about hearing accessibility?

### Buying Signals

**High intent:**
- "We can't find court reporters"
- "Our backlog is [X] months for transcripts"
- "We're renovating/building a new courthouse"

## EHS Manager

**Who they are:** Manages OSHA compliance, incident investigations, safety training.

**Title variations to look for:**
- EHS Director (Environment, Health & Safety)
- Safety Manager
- Director of Safety
- Plant Safety Manager

**Reports to:** VP of Operations, COO, VP of Manufacturing

**Budget authority:** $50K - $500K annually

### Pain Points

| Pain | Emotional Impact | How We Solve It |
|------|------------------|-----------------|
| Proving training was actually delivered | Compliance anxiety | Video documentation as proof |
| Incident investigations without video evidence | Legal liability | Multi-angle video capture |
| OSHA audit readiness documentation gaps | Stress, regulatory risk | Video evidence of safety training |

### Hot Buttons

- "Video proof of safety training delivery for OSHA"
- "One OSHA violation can cost $100K-$500K+"
- "Audit-ready documentation package"
- "Training consistency across all shifts"

### Discovery Questions

1. How do you currently document safety training compliance?
2. Have you had OSHA violations related to documentation gaps?
3. When an incident occurs, how do you document and investigate it?
4. How often do you face OSHA audits or inspections?

### Buying Signals

**High intent:**
- "We had an OSHA violation or citation"
- "We're preparing for an OSHA audit"
- "Leadership is asking us to improve documentation"

## Law Firm IT Director

**Who they are:** Manages deposition room technology and confidential recording.

**Title variations to look for:**
- Director of Information Technology (Law Firm)
- Legal Tech Manager
- Director of Legal Operations

**Reports to:** Managing Partner, COO, Director of Administration

**Budget authority:** $50K - $500K

### Pain Points

| Pain | Emotional Impact | How We Solve It |
|------|------------------|-----------------|
| Confidential case footage in cloud = discovery risk | Compliance/liability risk | Local recording - zero cloud exposure |
| Chain of custody integrity for evidence | Legal liability | Single device, tamper-evident recording |
| Video sync issues for credibility review | Professional risk | Perfect synchronization between angles |

### Hot Buttons

- "Local recording - confidential case materials never touch cloud"
- "No cloud = no discovery risk for sensitive IP/product cases"
- "Chain of custody ready - single device, single recording"
- "No subscription fees - fixed hardware cost"

### Discovery Questions

1. How are you capturing depositions today?
2. Do you have multiple deposition rooms across offices?
3. Are you recording locally or using cloud storage?
4. How do you handle multi-angle capture for credibility assessment?

### Buying Signals

**High intent:**
- "We're building/upgrading deposition rooms"
- "We need multi-angle video capability for trials"
- "We're concerned about cloud storage for confidential cases"
